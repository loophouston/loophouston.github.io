Episode 1: Lloyd Kirchner

Lloyd Kirchner is a friend and a man of many talents. He’s a...

  • marathon runner
  • improv jazz musician
  • entrepreneur
  • manager
  • Montessori parent and
  • race car driver

But like a lot of accomplished Houstonians, his story is not well known so I knew he would make a perfect subject for the first Loop Houston podcast.

In this podcast, Lloyd talks about:

  • Growing up in Houston through middle school, high school, and college (at Rice University)
  • Starting his professional career at a big integrated energy company in 1985.
  • Surviving the bottom of oil economy with oil at $8 per barrel in 1986 (“my boss said… I didn’t keep you because you were the smartest guy on the payroll, you were the cheapest.” :-))
  • Getting an MBA from University of Chicago
  • Joining as an early employee at micro-turbine innovator, Capstone Turbine Corp , and helping to take them public to a $7.5B IPO in 2000, just before the dot-com bubble burst (34:22, 39:44)
  • How lead engineers of Capstone Turbines went on to be central employees at SpaceX and Tesla (TSLA) and other leading companies today.
  • Moved back to Houston and co-founded Commonwealth Engineering in 2003, growing it to ...
  • Building the company culture at Commonwealth that set them apart and made them exceptional.
  • How progressive companies embrace the work habit and motivational differences of “millennials”
  • Should new graduates start a company or go to work at a well established company? (1:15:30)
  • Lloyd’s new venture, Tidal Wave, in the corporate health and safety software space.
  • Lloyd’s hobbies as an improv jazz musician, playing the keyboard and the bass.
  • Racing cars at raceways in California like Button Willow, Sears Point (1:39:30)
